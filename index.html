Our Story

Born on the Streets.
Forged in the Kitchen.

Shaolin Slurp didn't come from a culinary school or a Food Network pilot. It came from late nights, dog-eared VHS tapes, and a broth that took three days to get right.

Inspired by the discipline of the 36th Chamber, the raw energy of Wu-Tang street culture, and the cinematic grit of kung-fu grindhouse cinema — every bowl we serve is a statement. Tonkotsu simmered low and slow. Miso aged with intention. Greens treated with the same respect as steel.

This truck rolls as a moving dojo. Step up, eat deep, and walk away changed.
